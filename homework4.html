Reflection

The first image dives into global warming and the downfall of a world drowning in its own neglect. I wanted the people who see this image to feel the panic and anxiety. The woman’s cry embodies all the frustration, fear, and helplessness. The half-submerged traffic sign warns of danger, but it’s too late. Every element, from the cool blueish tones to the distortion of the waves, depicts a suffocating silence. I used Screen and Soft Light blending modes to blend the human skin with the sea’s texture. All the images were sourced from Unsplash, but through layering, tone shifts, and blending, I wanted the viewer to see the world is both screaming and sinking at the same time..

The second image explores hikikomori culture in Japan. In Japanese, the word for social withdrawal syndrome is hikikomori (引きこもり or ひきこもり). It refers to a person who is a severe shut-in, which is happening to more and more people in Japan. I wanted someone who looks at this image to feel suffocated, like being swallowed by their own thoughts. The overlapping Japanese calligraphy represents the noise of expectations and traditions. I used Multiply and Overlay blending modes to merge the background and figure, letting the colors bleed together like fading memories. The dim green light and rough textures were intentional, I wanted them to echo the loneliness, fear, and strange comfort that come from disappearing inside one's own world.
